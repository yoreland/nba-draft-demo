This reinforces a core lesson: **trust the aggregated market consensus over individual reporter scoops**.

## Key Conclusions

1. **Consensus aggregation is extremely powerful for top picks.** Perfect 5/5 at the top shows that when multiple sources agree, the market is very efficient.

2. **The long tail (picks 15-30) is inherently noisy.** Average displacement of 5.3 picks in this range reflects the reality of trades, team-specific needs, and workout-driven decisions that no public model can predict.

3. **Never override market consensus with individual reporter scoops.** The manual override based on Stein/Woo reports was wrong. Aggregated data beat insider information.

4. **Widen the candidate pool.** Missing 3 players entirely (all seniors) suggests the model needs a broader initial pool, possibly 40-50 players instead of 30.

5. **90% coverage is strong** but can be improved. Only 3/30 picks were complete surprises.

6. **20% exact match rate across all 30 picks** is a respectable result for a model built and validated in a single development sprint.
